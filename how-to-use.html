How to Use Mr. Wlah

Mr. Wlah (Write Like A Human) is a Flash-inspired web application that transforms AI-generated content into human-like text. Here's how to use it effectively:

Getting Started

1 Input Your Text

You have two options for inputting text:

  • Paste AI-generated text directly into the input area
  • Upload a document (.txt, .pdf, or .doc file)
Tip: For best results, use text that is grammatically correct but sounds too formal or robotic.

2 Select a Tone

Choose the desired tone for your transformed text:

  • Casual: Relaxed, everyday conversation style
  • Professional: Polished, business-appropriate tone
  • Scientific: Technical, research-oriented language
  • Educational: Clear, instructional style
  • Engaging: Captivating, attention-grabbing approach
  • Humorous: Light-hearted, witty tone
  • Storytelling: Narrative, immersive style

3 Transform Your Text

Click the "Transform Text" button to process your content. The application will:

  • Add personal experiences and anecdotes
  • Vary sentence structure for natural flow
  • Include colloquialisms and idioms
  • Add rhetorical questions for engagement
  • Maintain a consistent voice and tone

Using the Transformed Text

4 Review and Edit

The transformed text appears in the output section. Read through it to ensure it meets your needs and make any necessary adjustments.

5 Copy or Download

Once satisfied with the results, you can:

  • Click "Copy" to copy the text to your clipboard
  • Click "Download" to save the text as a file in your preferred format (.txt, .doc, or .pdf)

Examples

Before Transformation:

The process of neural encoding is fundamental to understanding brain function. Neurons transform sensory inputs into electrical signals through a complex series of biochemical reactions.

After Transformation (Scientific Tone):

Upon analysis of the available data, several patterns emerge. From my laboratory observations over the past decade, I've found that the process of neural encoding is indeed fundamental to understanding brain function. Isn't it fascinating how neurons transform sensory inputs into electrical signals? This happens through a complex series of biochemical reactions that I've spent countless hours documenting under various experimental conditions.

After Transformation (Casual Tone):

Hey, I've been thinking about this quite a bit lately. You know how it goes, right? Sometimes you just can't help but wonder about the process of neural encoding. It's really the key to getting how our brains work. I remember watching neurons do their thing in lab - they take all these sensory inputs and somehow turn them into electrical signals through this wild chemistry experiment happening in our heads. Pretty cool stuff!

Tips for Best Results

  • Use text that is at least a paragraph long for better context
  • Select a tone that's appropriate for your target audience
  • For technical content, the Scientific or Educational tone works best
  • For marketing or blog content, try the Engaging or Storytelling tone
  • Review the output and make minor adjustments as needed
